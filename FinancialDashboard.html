1. Spend Trends

Inpatient: A reduction in purchases was observed, primarily attributed to a $200,627.00 net 340B wholesaler credit, resulting from the correction of the 4C location code.

Outpatient: Significant reduction in Specialty purchases, which is more aligned with the historical monthly average. WAC purchases decreased, contributing to overall cost containment.

Walgreens: A decrease of $215,643.23 in spending was observed, with most of the reduction attributed to HIV medications and decreased spending on GLP-1 medications.

Primary Care Clinics: High spending in October, mainly due to increased demand for influenza and co-administered vaccines. November data is incomplete due to missing IDB information.

Registry: An increase in IP spending due to Soliant rebilling two invoices into November.

5. Miscellaneous Updates

Clinics

  • Floor stock updates in several clinics.
  • OBOT implementing cheaper alternative Methadone starting this week.
  • Switching to Menveo after the current doses of MenQuadfi run out.
  • Decrease in Flublok administration, requiring a strategy for next year.

DSCSA Updates

  • All policies and standard work are in place.
  • Training completed for most IP pharmacists.
  • TraceLink feeds 80% of eligible data successfully; Cencora's data is delayed.

  • Rebate pilot and MFP enrollment completed with Beacon.
  • Contract pharmacy process review for impacted patients.

WCPC Updates
